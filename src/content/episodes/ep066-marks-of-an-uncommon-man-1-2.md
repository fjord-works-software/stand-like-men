#1 – Marks of an Uncommon Man: Living From, Not For.
In the very first episode of Marks of an Uncommon Man, we explore one of the foundational differences between common living and uncommon living: identity. Most men spend their lives striving for approval, validation, success, and significance—but the uncommon man understands he is meant to live from acceptance, not for it. Anchored in Romans 12 and Colossians 3, this episode challenges men to stop performing for worth and begin operating from the finished work of Christ.
We discuss what it means to live from rest instead of exhaustion, from sonship instead of insecurity, and from heaven’s perspective instead of the world’s pressure. This conversation lays the groundwork for becoming a man who is transformed by God, rooted in identity, and unshaken by the opinions or expectations of others.
 
#2 – Marks of an Uncommon Man: He Knows Whose He Is.
What stabilizes a man when pressure, failure, obscurity, or warfare come? In this episode of Marks of an Uncommon Man, we dive into the power of belonging and identity through the Father. An uncommon man is not constantly trying to prove who he is through performance, titles, or accomplishments—he already knows whose he is. Rooted in 1 John 3:1, this episode unpacks the deep confidence and steadiness that comes from being named, loved, and claimed by God.
We talk about how identity before assignment changes the way a man handles leadership, adversity, and purpose. While the common man searches for worth through recognition and achievement, the uncommon man walks in quiet confidence because his identity was settled long before his success ever was.
